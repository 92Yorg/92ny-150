Celebrating

150

Years

The 92nd Street Y, New York was founded in 1874. As we begin our 150th anniversary celebration, join us for a year of talks, performances, poetry, concerts and dance, inspired by the icons who made our halls their home.

1883

Emma Lazarus, whose poem “The New Colossus” gave voice to the Statue of Liberty, teaches English to immigrants at 92NY.

1930

The new building opens on 92nd Street and Lexington Avenue.

1935

Martha Graham, Doris Humphrey, Hanya Holm and Charles Weidman develop modern dance at 92NY’s new Dance Center.

1960

Alvin Ailey premieres Revelations on the Kaufmann Concert Hall stage.

1964

Truman Capote debuts In Cold Blood at 92NY.

1974

Nineteen-year-old cellist Yo-Yo Ma gives his first 92NY recital.

1979

NThe country’s first Parenting Center opens at 92NY.

2012

Giving Tuesday is created at 92NY.
